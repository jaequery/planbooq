Task: Landing page variations for Planbooq — the multiplayer kanban "cockpit" for vibe coders running 10+ AI agent tickets in parallel. Bar: Each variant must land at a level a Linear/Resend/Vercel founder would quote-tweet. Side-by-side, the four must argue different theories of what the product is, not merely color-palette one template. Axes: register × proof-method. Click Pick this to ship that variant; Redo to send back; Kill to drop. Picks are written to picks.jsonl and shown in your terminal.

01terminal-utility

● pass fidelity 9.5 / craft 9 / diff 10

The page refuses to be a marketing page. The hero is rendered as a CLI session: a fake prompt, $ planbooq status, a live-feeling tail of 10 tickets moving through columns as ANSI-color rows. Body is JetBrains Mono throughout. Sections labeled ## features, ## pricing like a man page. Copy reads like commit messages — declarative, short, no superlatives. The thesis: "you're a developer; we'll talk to you like one."

Thesis fidelity: 9.5 Craft: 9 Differentiation: 10

What works

  • Hero CLI tail with ANSI green/amber row semantics — the most product-honest hero in months.
  • ## why as a real man-page (NAME / DESCRIPTION / SYNOPSIS with planbooq [--worktrees|--byok] <ticket>).
  • ## install covers all three deploy modes (DMG / web / CLI) without one foregrounded.
  • ## see also footer reads exactly like the SEE ALSO section of a man page.
  • Manpage footer planbooq(1) 2026-05-16 Built in San Francisco. Detail-perfect.

What fails

  • Nothing material. The Next dev "N — 2 issues" badge in the corner is a dev artifact, would not ship.

02editorial-broadsheet

● pass fidelity 9 / craft 9.5 / diff 10

A long-form manifesto landing in the lineage of Stripe Press, Read.cv, and Browser Company essays. Hero is a single editorial sentence at clamp-large on cream paper with deep ink. Drop cap on the lead paragraph. Pull quotes mid-page. Body in Spectral 18px/30. The thesis: "vibe coding deserves a manifesto, not a SaaS landing."

Thesis fidelity: 9 Craft: 9.5 Differentiation: 10

What works

  • Hero sentence "You are running ten AI sessions in ten terminals — and you are losing nine of them." — screenshot-and-quote-tweet material.
  • Real ::first-letter drop cap, ~5 lines tall.
  • Pull quote pulled FROM the body, italic, double-ruled.
  • Chapter titles ("The false promise of one ticket at a time", "Attention is the only finite resource") read like a Stripe Press essay.
  • Masthead PLANBOOQ · A KANBAN MANIFESTO · ISSUE 01 · MAY 2026 sets the right tone.

What fails

  • The board still-life below "Attention is the only finite resource" is a competent HTML/CSS sketch but lacks the photographic textural quality the brief asked for. Not enough to demote.

03kinetic-board

● pass (after 2 redos) fidelity 8 / craft 8 / diff 10

The hero IS the product, running. Cards animate through five columns at speed: a fake commit arrives every ~1.2s, a Building card pulses, a Completed card fades. Pure black with a single electric-green accent used like a Bond title sequence. The thesis: "prove parallelism by showing it." Note: in static screenshots motion is implied — the live page shows continuous card flow.

Thesis fidelity: 8 Craft: 8 Differentiation: 10

What works

  • Hero board with 5 columns and real-feeling cards (avatars + PR numbers). Building column glows green — accent rationed.
  • "The Problem" section: three terminal mocks with spinners + "Run all three. Simultaneously." in accent green. Shows-don't-tells.
  • "Three steps. Everything else is noise." tight and confident.
  • Live Stats counters reserve green for the middle "Live agents" number only — disciplined inside a live-counter feature.
  • Final CTA "Ten tickets. Ten worktrees. One glance." with neon-green Download button — only place green goes large.

What fails

  • Burned 2 redos on plumbing (module-level seq counter caused hydration mismatch; IntersectionObserver re-hide broke headless capture). Final fixes correct but craft fell slightly behind the others.
  • CTA pair "Download for Mac" / "Start in browser" is a soft violation of the no-pair rule, defended only by being real product paths rather than generic.

04swiss-grid-publisher

● pass fidelity 9.5 / craft 10 / diff 10

Strict 12-col Swiss grid, Inter Tight at editorial spacing. White, ink, single rationed oxblood accent reserved for one mark per viewport. Sections numbered 01 / Throughput, 02 / Worktrees, labeled in tiny tracked caps. Type and grid carry the design — no animation, no flourish, no shadows. A Müller-Brockmann-style poster visualizes the 10 parallel lanes purely with vertical rules and timing marks. The thesis: "restraint as the product's argument."

Thesis fidelity: 9.5 Craft: 10 Differentiation: 10

What works

  • Hero "The cockpit for vibe coders running ten tickets at once." — Apple-AirPods-page-tier composition.
  • Oxblood underline on "Start building" is the only accent in the hero viewport. Rationed.
  • Numbered sections (01–06) hold discipline top to bottom.
  • The 10-lane Müller-Brockmann poster with one oxblood bar (Lane 03) is the strongest single typographic composition in the lineup.
  • "Reviewing ten tickets is not harder than reviewing one. It is just ten glances instead of one." — a sentence I'd put on a poster.

What fails

  • Lanes poster uses CSS grid bars rather than SVG; trades a touch of typographic precision for resilience. Acceptable.
